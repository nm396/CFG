Nutrition

It can be useful to keep checking in on all the vitamins and minerals that your body needs. Hopefully this can help you check in with each group. We keep a chart in our kitchen for easy reference which you can find here.

Fats

Vital to get Omega 3. Essential for brain and nerve function.

  • Olive Oil
  • Linseed Oil
  • Rapeseed Oil
  • Avocados
  • Nuts
  • Green Leafy Veg

Protein

For growth and repair of body tissue.
For hormones and enzymes.

  • Beans and Lentils
  • Beansprouts
  • Tofu
  • Soya Milk
  • Wholegrains
  • Seeds
  • Nuts
  • Hummous

Vitamins

A

Needed for bones and teeth, sight, growth and repair.
Also an antioxidant

  • Carrots
  • Spinach
  • Red and Yellow peppers
  • Watercress
  • Tomatoes
  • Green leafy veg
  • Dried Apricots
  • Mango
  • (Brightly coloured fruit and veg)

D

Needed for Calcium use.
Healthy bones and teeth.
May need to supplement in the winter

  • Sunlight on the skin
  • Fortified Cereals
  • Fortified Margarine
  • Fortified Soya Milk
  • (D2 is animal free)

E

Used to protect against disease and for skin health
Antioxidant

  • Olive Oil
  • Tomatoes
  • Avocado
  • Apples
  • Carrots
  • Nuts and Seeds
  • Wholegrains

K

Fights infection.
For blood clotting and healthy bones

  • Green leafy veg
  • Kelp
  • Lettuce
  • Broccoli
  • Peas
  • Lentils

B group

B1 (Thiamin), B2 (Riboflavin), B3 (Niacin), B6 (Pyridoxin)

Important for energy, using fats and protein.
For cell growth and nervous system

  • Beansprouts
  • Avocado
  • Wholegrains
  • Nuts
  • Mushrooms
  • Bananas
  • Oranges
  • Yeast Extract
  • Beans and Lentils
  • Breen Leafy Veg

B12

Used in nerve formation and cell production.
Deficiency can lead to anaemia

  • Fortified Soya Milk
  • Fortified Cereals
  • Fortified Margarine
  • Soya "Meat"
  • Yeast Extract

C

Used to fight infection and heal wounds
Antioxidant

  • Green leafy veg
  • Broccoli
  • Parsley
  • Potatoes
  • Frozen Peas
  • Oranges
  • Blackcurrents
  • Kiwi
  • Mango
