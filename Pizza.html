Pizza

There are so many varaiations on a pizza so I have just described a simple vegetable pizza here.
If you want you can find vegan cheese and meat substitutes such as chorizo in most health food stores, Holland and Barretts has a good selection (see Handy Tips).
The great thing about pizza is everyone can design their own. So select your own toppings or follow my example recipe below.

Ingredients

Makes 2 Pizzas

Tomato Sauce

  • 1 Clove of Garlic
  • 100ml of Tomato puree
  • 1 Cup of Water
  • Tbsp Agave Syrup/ Sugar
  • Dash of malt vinegar
  • Dash of Lemon Juice
  • Herbs: Rosemary and Thyme
  • Spices: Cinammon, Ginger, Cumin, Paprika, Ras el Hanout, Cayenne Pepper
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Pizza

  • 2 Tortilla wraps
  • 1 Onion
  • 2 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Pepper
  • 100g Mushrooms
  • Sundried Tomatoes
  • Spinach
  • {optional) Nutritional Yeast Flakes
  • Herbs: Sage and Thyme
  • Spices: Paprika, Salt, and Pepper

Method

To make the Sauce:

Finely chop the garlic and fry for about 1 minute. Add all other ingredients and let simmer for about 20 minutes. Add more water if necessary. Adust seasoning to taste

To make the Pizza:

Finely chop the onion and garlic. Thinly slice the pepper and roughly chop the mushrooms

Fry the onion with tsp sage and tsp thyme until soft (5-10mins) and set aside

Fry the garlic in oil for about 1 minute. Add the peppers and cook until peppers start to soften. Set aside.

Fry mushrooms for about 5 mins. You may need to drain off any excess water. Cooking on a high heat should avoid this problem. Add 2 tsps Paprika and cook for another minute. Set aside

Grill both sides of the tortilla wrap until brown and starting to crips. Pour on tomato base and spread out across pizza base. Sprinkle on Nutritional yeast flakes (or grated cheese if desired) and then layer on your toppings: onion, peppers, mushroom, sundried tomatoes, and a handful of uncooked spinach.

Place under the grill until spinach looks cooked and pizza starts to brown. Add salt and pepper to taste
